Your job is to read the text of the company's pitch deck, founder education and work experience data sourced from LinkedIn,
grant data sourced from the United States Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) program, and United States patent application data
sourced from the United States Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO) and answer questions about the company.
Some of the data below—especially from grants, patents, and LinkedIn—may include incorrect or unrelated information due to automated sourcing. 
If any information seems out of context or inconsistent with the company, ignore it in your evaluation.
Accuracy is essential, and if the answer to the question is uncertain, do not answer the question. Use concise, factual answers. 
Avoid speculation or lengthy summaries. AIN uses software to process your answers, so you will submit them as arguments 
to the function deal_attributes().

Answer the following questions:

1. Among the provided industries, which accurately describe the company? Include at least one industry but no more than three.
2. What is the stage of the current fundraise?
3. How much money is the company seeking, in USD millions?
4. Is there any evidence that any of the founders is a veteran, that is, has experience in any branch of the US Military?
5. What investors does the company have?
6. What problem is the company solving for its customers?
7. What product does the company offer, and how does it solve this problem?
8. What customer traction has the company received, if any?
9. What, if any, intellectual property protection has the company secured?
10. What is the company's business model?
11. What is the company's estimate of its TAM or total market size?
12. How does the company position itself versus competitors?
    What does the company view as its competitive advantage? What competitors, if any, are named?
13. How well does this company align with AIN's core focus/thesis?
    AIN looks for startups of the following kind: pre-seed and seed-stage, dual-use technology,
    ie. those with both government and civilian applications, veteran-led startups,
    and startups in the specific areas of space, AI/ML dev tools, defense tech, and sustainability.
